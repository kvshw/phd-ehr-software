üî¥ Research Problem

Why Current EHR Systems Fail Healthcare Professionals

üò´

One-Size-Fits-All Design

Identical interfaces for cardiologists, neurologists, nurses, and emergency physicians despite vastly different clinical workflows and priorities.

  • No specialty customization
  • Fixed section ordering
  • Generic quick actions
‚è∞

Time Inefficiency

Studies show clinicians spend 2+ hours daily navigating EHR interfaces instead of focusing on direct patient care.

  • 44% of physician time on EHR
  • Repeated navigation patterns
  • No learning from usage
üîî

Alert Fatigue & Trust

Generic AI suggestions lead to high ignore rates (up to 90%+), reducing trust in clinical decision support systems.

  • Irrelevant alerts
  • No personalization
  • Declining acceptance rates
üí°

Research Question

"How can we create an EHR interface that automatically adapts to individual clinician workflows, specialties, and preferences using self-adaptive systems?"

üéØ Proposed Solution

Self-Adaptive EHR Using MAPE-K Feedback Loop

What is MAPE-K?

MAPE-K is an architectural pattern for autonomous computing systems, introduced by IBM in 2003. It enables software to self-manage by continuously monitoring, analyzing, planning, and executing adaptations based on a knowledge base.

Key Benefits

  • Continuous learning from user behavior
  • Real-time interface optimization
  • Specialty-aware customization
  • Reduced cognitive load
  • Improved AI suggestion relevance

üéì Research Contributions

Novel Contributions to Healthcare Informatics

1

Self-Adaptive UI Framework for EHR

First implementation of complete MAPE-K feedback loop specifically designed for Electronic Health Record interface adaptation. Addresses the gap in applying autonomous computing principles to clinical software.

2

Specialty-Aware Personalization

Novel approach combining static specialty knowledge with learned behavioral patterns. Clinicians get sensible defaults that improve over time based on individual usage.

3

Explainable AI Integration

Clinical decision support with evidence-based explanations including GRADE levels, citations, and clinical guidelines. Addresses the "black box" problem in healthcare AI.

4

Continuous Learning Engine

Feedback loop that processes clinician interactions with AI suggestions to progressively improve relevance and reduce alert fatigue.

5

Research Analytics Platform

Built-in metrics and data export capabilities for HCI researchers to study adaptation effectiveness, user behavior, and clinical decision support acceptance.
